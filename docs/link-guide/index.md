# The Seattle Link guide

Sound Transit's **Link light rail** is the spine of regional transit in the Seattle area. As of 2026 it runs two lines and 38 stations, from Lynnwood in the north to Federal Way in the south, and east across Lake Washington to Redmond.

## Two lines, one shared spine

Both lines share the central corridor through downtown Seattle — from Lynnwood down to International District/Chinatown, trains of either line stop at the same 13 stations. South of downtown the <span class="line-1">1 Line</span> continues alone toward the airport and Federal Way. From International District the <span class="line-2">2 Line</span> swings east across the Interstate 90 floating bridge to Bellevue and Redmond.

On the [walksheds.xyz](https://walksheds.xyz) map the two lines are drawn in their official colors and, through the shared segment, offset slightly — the <span class="line-1">1 Line</span> nudged west, the <span class="line-2">2 Line</span> east — so you can tell them apart.

## How the stations are numbered

Each station has a two-digit **stop code**. Westlake, in the heart of downtown, is `50`. The numbers climb as you head **south on the 1 Line** and **east on the 2 Line**, and fall as you go north. Gaps in the numbering are reserved for stations not yet built. Two stations share code `54` — Stadium on the 1 Line and Judkins Park on the 2 Line — which is why the guide always pairs a code with its line color.
