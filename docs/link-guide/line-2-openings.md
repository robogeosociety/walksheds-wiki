# 2 Line station openings <span class="roundel roundel-2">2</span>

Every <span class="line-2">2 Line</span> station in the order it opened. The 2 Line grew unusually — it began on the Eastside with no link to Seattle, and only reached the [shared spine](line-1.md) when the floating-bridge crossing opened in 2026. Open any station on the [map](https://walksheds.xyz) to see its walkshed.

## 2024-04-27 — the starter line

Eight Eastside stations opened as a self-contained line, with no Seattle connection yet — riders used it within Bellevue and Redmond.

| Station | A short walk away |
| --- | --- |
| South Bellevue `56` | A large park-and-ride beside the Mercer Slough Nature Park. |
| East Main `57` | The southern edge of downtown Bellevue and the Surrey Downs neighborhood. |
| Bellevue Downtown `58` | Downtown Bellevue — one of the densest downtowns in the state — Bellevue Downtown Park, and the Bellevue Collection. |
| Wilburton `59` | The Wilburton area and the Eastrail trail; a district planned for major growth. |
| Spring District/120th `60` | The Spring District — former industrial land rebuilt as a dense neighborhood, home to REI's headquarters. |
| Bel-Red/130th `61` | The Bel-Red corridor, mid-transformation from warehouses to housing and offices. |
| Overlake Village `62` | The Overlake area near the Microsoft campus, with new apartments and retail. |
| Redmond Technology `63` | The Microsoft campus and the Redmond technology corridor. |

## 2025-05-10 — Downtown Redmond

| Station | A short walk away |
| --- | --- |
| Marymoor Village `64` | Near Marymoor Park, the region's big concert-and-recreation park. |
| Downtown Redmond `65` | The eastern end of the 2 Line — downtown Redmond and Redmond Town Center. |

## 2026-03-28 — across the lake

The "Crosslake Connection" carried the 2 Line over Lake Washington on the I-90 floating bridge — the world's first light rail to do so — adding two new stations and finally joining the Eastside to Seattle.

| Station | A short walk away |
| --- | --- |
| Judkins Park `54` | The first 2 Line stop in Seattle — the Central District and Mount Baker neighborhoods, the I-90 lid parks and trail. |
| Mercer Island `55` | The Mercer Island town center, mid-lake, with bus connections from across the island. |

!!! note "The shared spine"
    On the same day, 2 Line trains began running through downtown Seattle and north to **Lynnwood**, over the 13 [shared-spine stations](line-1-openings.md) (Westlake, Capitol Hill, UW, Northgate, Lynnwood, and the rest). Those stations had opened years earlier with the 1 Line — 2026-03-28 is when the *2 Line* started serving them.
