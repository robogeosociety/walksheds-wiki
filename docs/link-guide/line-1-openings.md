# 1 Line station openings <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span>

Every <span class="line-1">1 Line</span> station in the order it opened — the order the line actually grew in — with what's a short walk away. Open any of these on the [map](https://walksheds.xyz) to see the real walkshed.

Stations marked <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span><span class="roundel roundel-2">2</span> are on the shared spine and are also served by the [2 Line](line-2.md).

## 2009-07-18 — the first trains

Central Link opened between downtown Seattle and Tukwila, instantly connecting downtown to the Rainier Valley.

| Station | Lines | A short walk away |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Westlake `50` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span><span class="roundel roundel-2">2</span> | The downtown retail core, Westlake Center, the monorail; Pike Place Market is a longer stroll. |
| Symphony `51` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span><span class="roundel roundel-2">2</span> | Benaroya Hall and the Seattle Symphony, the downtown office core, the central library. |
| Pioneer Square `52` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span><span class="roundel roundel-2">2</span> | Seattle's oldest neighborhood — historic brick blocks, Smith Tower, galleries, the ferry terminal. |
| International District/Chinatown `53` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span><span class="roundel roundel-2">2</span> | The Chinatown-International District, Uwajimaya, Seattle's busiest transit hub; the stadiums are close. |
| Stadium `54` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span> | Lumen Field and T-Mobile Park — the football and baseball stadiums. |
| SODO `55` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span> | The SODO industrial district, Starbucks headquarters, warehouses and makers. |
| Beacon Hill `56` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span> | The heart of Beacon Hill, reached by deep elevators; Jefferson Park is nearby. |
| Mount Baker `57` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span> | The Mount Baker hub on Rainier Avenue, Franklin High School. |
| Columbia City `58` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span> | One of the most walkable stops on the line — Columbia City's historic business district and farmers market. |
| Othello `60` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span> | The Othello and New Holly neighborhoods, among the most diverse in Seattle. |
| Rainier Beach `61` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span> | The Rainier Beach neighborhood; Kubota Garden is a walk to the east. |
| Tukwila International Blvd `63` | <span class="roundel roundel-1">1</span> | Elevated beside International Boulevard, with hotels and bus connections. |
